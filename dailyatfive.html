Background

  1. Having worked on analyzing the conditions of domestic workers as part of my economics project, the findings gathered at the time motivated me to to pursue a solution for this apparently grave but often ignored issue.
  2. With nuclear families flourishing in major cities of the country and people finding it difficult to manage their homes and the workplace pressure, the rise of demand of domestic help for households in cities has inevitably risen.
  3. Currently, a lot of players are coming into the market but the adoption is still slow. This is mainly attributed to a lack of satisfactory service. But the focus for MaidAid is to provide domestic help when you need them the most. This usually happens when the employed maid runs off or takes a unexpected leave leaving the consumers helpless. The bigger picture we want to cater to is to provide due recognition to domestic workers.

Research

Conducted paper based and survey and one on one interviews.

Interviews were conducted with 64 people from majorly two areas : R.K.Puram and Vasant Kunj.

Formulated the questionnaire. Have a look at the entire questionnaire here.

Findings

Major problems in hiring:

  1. Lack of Options
  2. Irregular Service
  3. Unsatisfactory Work.
  4. High fees charged by Help/Agencies.

Current process of hiring is mostly via asking aroung in their neighborhoods. About 62% of families had hired domestic help on a part time basis, suggesting a trend for part time workers.

The most in demand services were that of House Cleaning and Cooking.About 65% consumers were willing to pay a premium in return for better services.

Storyboard

This is a story about a working married woman Reena. Reena has 2 kids and works in a private insurance firm. It's a monday morning and she has to rush to her office but sadly her maid hasn't come yet.

Reena is waiting for her maid.It's already 9am and she has to leave for her workplace by 10. Nothing seems to be going right for her and this has just aggravated her agitation.

Her maid hasn't come and she decides to leave for office. Distraught and helpless at the same time, the high fees just adds to the suffering. Around 10:30, her maid calls her and tells her that her child is sick and she can't come for the next 4 days.

Fustrated, Reena downloads the app MaidAid which was referred to her her neighbour. She opens the app and registers herself on the app.

Next, she fills in her preferences : the type of work she wants to get done, the number of days she wants the service, the timings etc.

Now she is able to view all the domestic helps working near her house. She discovers that one of the domestic helps is working for her friend Sheena. She calls up Sheena to ask about her the quality of service she is getting.

Satisfied but not completely assured, she decides to put in a call request on the app. The help calls her within 5 mins and Reena gets all her doubts cleared. She books maid for the next 4 days and goes to sleep that night free from the stress of managing her help.
